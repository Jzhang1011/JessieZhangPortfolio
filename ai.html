MIA — Marketing Intelligence Assistant

Audience: Marketing teams, analytics partners, and cross-functional stakeholders

BUSINESS NEED

Marketing knowledge was fragmented across dashboards, spreadsheets, SOPs, and people. Finding the right KPI, report, or owner often depended on asking the right person.

WHAT MIA DOES

  • Finds dashboards and data locations
  • Explains KPI definitions and marketing processes
  • Surfaces stakeholder ownership and org context
  • Supports onboarding and routine self-service questions

SOURCES

  • Static marketing SOPs and reference guides
  • KPI and dashboard index
  • RACI charts and org structure materials
  • Access and permissions documentation
  • Role-based onboarding guides

GUARDRAILS

  • Source citations
  • No-inference rules unless explicitly requested
  • Role- and scenario-aware responses
  • Knowledge-base-only answers

USE CASES

  • Dashboard and KPI lookup
  • Stakeholder and org map lookup
  • Marketing process guidance
  • New-hire onboarding (30/60/90 day support)

OUTCOMES

  • Improved access to marketing intelligence
  • Reduced search time for common questions
  • Lower dependency on subject-matter experts
  • Strong fit for onboarding and self-service support

WHY IT MATTERS

MIA is not just a chatbot — it is a new way to scale marketing knowledge. Instead of expertise living only in people, it becomes accessible through a governed system.

MMM Copilot — Learning & Decision Support

Audience: Channel owners, revenue marketers, and teams new to MMM

BUSINESS NEED

MMM adoption often requires office hours, expert interpretation, and repeated training. Without support, new users struggle to understand outputs and apply them confidently.

WHAT MMM COPILOT DOES

  • Explains MMM concepts in plain language
  • Provides role-based guidance for channel owners and marketers
  • Supports learning through scenario-based examples
  • Helps users connect model outputs to practical decisions

SOURCES

  • MMM best practices guide
  • Role-based workflows
  • Decision scenarios and learning materials

GUARDRAILS

  • Role-based explanation depth
  • Scenario-based guidance for common decision patterns
  • No unsupported interpretation beyond documented guidance

USE CASES

  • MMM onboarding and capability building
  • Decision support for stakeholders unfamiliar with MMM
  • Self-service learning between training sessions

OUTCOMES

  • Faster ramp into MMM concepts and workflows
  • Lower enablement burden on experts
  • Higher confidence for non-expert users
  • Potential to scale training without scaling support headcount

WHY IT MATTERS

MMM Copilot shows how AI can accelerate capability adoption — turning expert-only knowledge into scalable, on-demand learning support for the broader organization.

The AI-native approach

Traditional model

  • Knowledge lives in people
  • Answers require meetings, pings, or office hours
  • Scaling support requires more expert time

AI-native model

  • Knowledge is structured and embedded in the product
  • Answers are self-service and always available
  • Expertise scales through systems, not just headcount
